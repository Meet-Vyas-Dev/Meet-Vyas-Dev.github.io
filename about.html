More About Me

Astrophysics and Cosmology

I am currently focused on Cosmology, Galactic Astrophysics, and Time-Domain Astronomy. I am particularly working in the areas of simulating light curves, numerically calculating the precession of orbits around compact objects, and parameter estimation using data analysis techniques for cosmological parameters. My work often involves high-performance computing and AI-driven data analysis. Being an astronomy enthusiast, I also enjoy stargazing and exploring the wonders of the night sky.

Mechanical Engineering

During my degree in Mechanical Engineering, I worked on a variety of projects in solar thermal energy, robotics integrated with AI, metallurgy, machines and mechanical systems, and setups for studying the behavior of electro-weak fishes. These experiences have given me a solid foundation in both theoretical and practical aspects of engineering.

Interdisciplinary Projects

I have contributed to a diverse range of projects, including the development of an IoT-based air quality sensor network, the design and implementation of advanced scheduling algorithms for university course timetabling, and the optimization of vehicle routing problems in computer science. My ability to integrate knowledge from multiple disciplines developed during my undergrad years has been a key asset in my work and research.

Philosophy

I have been exploring various philosophical discourses formally starting with anthropology of texts and literature and humanistic inquiry, where I followed the rise of science and the question of individualism that arises from it. I have been reading about various schools of thoughts including but not limited to nhilism, existentialism, absurdism, and stoicism. To answer some of the questions that arose from my inquiry with these schools, I have been focusing on Indian literature in the form of religious texts to understand the rich culture of my roots and to find meaning in this concious existence. My academic pursuits in this area have been enriched by my engagement with different schools of thought and my interest in the philosophy of science in which I am particularly interested in the epistemology of physics, especially in the context of cosmology and inflationary models.

Undergraduate Thesis - Ahmedabad University

Joshi as a part of my undergraduate thesis, where I worked on probing Primordial non-Gaussianity for different models of inflation, checking self consistency of models and performing parameter estimation for the amplitude of the non gaussianity using mcmc methods and testing the goodness of fit using chi-square analysis.

Supervised Informal Research Experience

Undergraduate Research Project - Neurophysiology Lab, Bioscience Research Laboratory, Ahmedabad University

I created a mechanical setup to test the foraging behavior of ghost knifefish (Apteronotus albifrons), under the guidance of Professor Rama Ratnam. I used infrared LEDs to mimic a nocturnal environment for the fishes, along with an ArduCam with an IR cut filter which was used to record the movement of the fishes and then mathematically modeled the same using ToxTrac.

Operations Research

Neural Network along with 3-D SLAM for developing an autonomous robot - Robotics

Designed and crafted a 10mm Acrylic chassis for a mobile robot. Developed an obstacle detection neural network using Roboflow, optimized for OpenCV AI Kit camera OAK-D. Integrated two L298N motor drivers with an Arduino Mega to control four Mecanum wheels, including the corresponding Arduino code. Implemented a 3D Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) using the depthai-sdk to premap and real-time remap the environment. Ongoing work includes integrating these components to create a fully autonomous robot, with plans to transition the main device controller from a laptop to an NVIDIA Jetson Nano. This was performed under the guidance of Professor Maryam Kaveshgar.
